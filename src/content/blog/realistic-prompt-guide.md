Creating photorealistic AI images requires specific prompt techniques. This guide will help you generate images that look like professional photographs.

## Key Elements of Realistic Prompts

### Camera Specifications
Mention specific camera terms:
- "Shot on Sony A7R IV"
- "85mm lens, f/1.8"
- "ISO 100, fast shutter speed"

### Lighting Descriptions
Lighting is crucial for realism:
- "Natural window lighting"
- "Three-point studio lighting"
- "Golden hour sunset light"
- "Soft diffused overhead light"

### Texture and Detail
Realism lives in the details:
- "Visible skin pores and texture"
- "Fine hair details"
- "Cloth fiber texture"
- "Surface imperfections"

## Example Realistic Prompts

**Portrait Photography:**
> "Professional headshot of a middle-aged businessman with salt-and-pepper beard, wearing a navy suit, natural office lighting, shallow depth of field, shot on 85mm lens"

**Product Photography:**
> "A luxury watch on a marble surface, macro photography, dramatic side lighting, reflections on polished metal, ultra detailed, commercial product photography"

**Landscape Photography:**
> "Misty morning at a mountain lake, pine forest reflection, golden sunrise light breaking through fog, National Geographic style photography"

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Over-describing**: Too many elements can confuse the AI
2. **Ignoring lighting**: Flat lighting kills realism
3. **Unrealistic combinations**: Physics still matters
4. **Forgetting negative prompts**: Exclude common artifacts

## Best Settings for Realism

For the most realistic results:
- Select the "Realistic" style
- Use 3:2 or 4:3 aspect ratios
- Include photography terminology in prompts
- Add quality modifiers
